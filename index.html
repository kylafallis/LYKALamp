III. Description of Invention: The LYKA Lamp

The **LYKA Lamp** is a functional microbial fuel cell (MFC)-powered lighting system that converts energy from the chemical bonds of organic compounds into electrical energy through the catalytic activity of microorganisms. It is engineered to integrate an MFC system into a practical lamp powered by an average family's organic waste.

The lamp was developed using **optimal MFC conditions**, including compost aged 12-18 months, a 1-Liter container, and carbon cloth electrodes spaced at 1 cm. It features a **custom-built step-up DC converter** and battery storage system to store the generated energy and power a 9V LED lightbulb.

This design makes small MFCs a sustainable, cost-effective lighting solution for households.

The custom circuit links the Anode/Cathode Chambers to a rechargeable battery and the lightbulb socket via power converter modules.

Performance Snapshot

  • **MFC Output (Optimal Test Conditions):** Average of 1.73 Watts (12-18 Month Compost, 1L Container, Carbon Cloth, 1 cm Spacing).
  • **Charge Time:** The lamp requires 11.95 hours to produce and store energy.
  • **Run Time:** Provides 2.43 hours of continuous light.
  • **Total Cost:** The materials cost for one LYKA Lamp is estimated at $16.77.
