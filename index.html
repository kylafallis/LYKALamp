The **LYKA Lamp**

Harnessing the Microbe: Renewable Power from Household Compost.

III. The Integrated Solution

The LYKA Lamp is the first fully integrated household device to convert organic waste directly into stable, usable electricity. It is an end-to-end decentralized energy solution, engineered to maximize microbial output and provide genuine functional light.

**$16.77** Total Material Cost
**11.95 hrs** Charge Time for Full Capacity
**2.43 hrs** Continuous Run Time (9V LED)

Engineered for sustainability, efficiency, and scale.

System Architecture

The system links the bio-anodic MFC chambers to a custom electronics suite to achieve stable power storage and delivery.

Circuit components include the **LM2596 Buck Power Convertor** and **TP4056 Charger Module** for efficient energy storage in the rechargeable battery.

II. Optimization Research: The 1.73 Watt Benchmark

The final design was informed by systematic comparative testing (Test 1 & 2) across critical variables to achieve the maximum output of 1.73 Watts per cell.

IV. Global Application & Economic Feasibility

The LYKA Lamp’s true potential lies in its ability to simultaneously address energy poverty, climate change, and household waste management.

Decentralized Energy

Provides a crucial, reliable lighting solution for the 770M+ people without access to traditional electricity. The simple, low-cost design makes it instantly accessible for **Off-Grid Communities** and **Disaster Relief**.

Waste Mitigation

Mitigates the environmental load of organic waste, which contributes to greenhouse gas emissions. In agricultural settings, MFCs can also reduce nutrient runoff, directly addressing issues like ocean **dead zones**.
