I. The Innovation: Compost to Current

The **LYKA Lamp** is a functional lighting system powered entirely by **Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs)** that utilize household compost. MFCs are bioreactors that convert the energy in chemical bonds of organic compounds into electrical energy via microorganisms under anaerobic conditions.

This project successfully developed an integrated system to generate, store, and convert this bio-energy into stable power for an LED lightbulb, offering a sustainable, cost-effective, and low-maintenance lighting solution.

**Hypothesis:** MFC energy from average US family compost is sufficient to run at least one LED light bulb and cut costs by more than $20 per year.

II. Optimization: Maximizing Bio-Energy Output

III. Engineering the Solution (Test 3)

The final design converted the low, variable MFC power into stable 9V light using a custom-built circuit.

Custom Circuitry

The MFC is connected to a circuit board featuring an LM2596 Buck Power Convertor and a TP4056 Charger Module. This system harvests the energy, manages the battery, and delivers stable power to the LED.

The lamp body and MFC housing were designed using SolidWorks and 3D printed with ABA filament.

LYKA Performance Metrics

  • **Peak Power (MFC):** 1.73 Watts.
  • **Energy Required:** 4.91 Watt-hours.
  • **Charge Time:** 11.95 hours of energy generation required to fill the battery.
  • **Run Time:** Provides 2.43 hours of continuous light.
  • **Material Cost:** Only **$16.77** per unit.
